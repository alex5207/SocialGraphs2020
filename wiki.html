Hollywood as a sign of the times

What if Hollywood could tell us about what's going on in the world? Imagine that the movies produced in Hollywood somehow reflect their time.

We apply a statistical measure called TF-IDF to identify the most important words in the movie descriptions for the movies in each decade. Then we compare the words with those used in the Wikipedia page for each decade (example here).

What we hope to find is that the words used in movie descriptions can be traced to events or sentiments of the real world.

... And now for some results

What is TF-IDF?

The Term-frequency/Inverted-document-frequency is a statistical measure that can be used to measure the importance of words in a pieces of text among other texts.

See more about the measure in our notebook or on wikipedia

The nature of the texts

Movie descriptions

Movie descriptions are generally storytelling. They contain descriptive words about the movie like describing the characters as young, a father etc.

Wikipedia pages
Wikipedia pages are objective by nature. The decade descriptions seek to summarize the decade in a compact and non-emotional way.
